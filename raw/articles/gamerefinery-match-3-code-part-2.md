# How to Crack the Match 3 Code? - Part 2

**Author:** Om Tandon, Innovation & UX Design Manager at Eaton
**Date:** 21 July 2020
**Source:** https://www.gamerefinery.com/how-to-crack-the-match-3-code-part-2/

## Introduction

Match 3 games are experiencing significant transformation driven by "maturing player behaviour and deepening game design." This analysis examines how the genre divides between Classic Match 3 (dominated by King and Playrix) and Meta Match 3, exploring new design directions and audience opportunities.

---

## Classic Match 3: King's Evolution

King is actively defending its market position through substantial reinvention of Candy Crush Saga, transforming it into "a much different beast than its original avatar 8 years ago."

### Secondary Meta Goals and Events

King introduced multiple engagement mechanics:

- **Social proof & community engagement events** featuring real players (predominantly women aged 35+)
- **Time-limited event levels** with themed gameplay
- **Leaderboard races** using small player pools to ensure competitive motivation
- **Scroll collection events** for special offers

These represent "soft-bodied versions" of mid-core and hard-core game mechanics, though questions remain about long-term effectiveness.

### Critical Issues

**1. Lack of Variable and Distinct Rewards**

The primary weakness centers on reward monotony. "All rewards are boosters & all boosters feel 'kind of' same in Candy Crush Saga." Players receive familiar booster combinations across different events, creating predictable dopamine patterns.

The article highlights how "players get all too familiar with these boosters" through:
- Daily reward samplers
- In-game board cascades featuring common boosters
- Identical reward structures across progression tiers

This consistency dilutes perceived value, particularly for veteran players.

**2. Absence of Rarity Systems**

Current boosters lack distinctive identity compared to iconic rewards like the "Mighty Eagle" in original Angry Birds. That booster commanded value through rarity and comprehensive power, whereas CCS boosters are distributed too frequently.

**3. Single Currency Economy Limitations**

Candy Crush Saga operates on a "single premium currency economy (gold bars)" inherited from its legacy design. This architectural constraint forces nearly all rewards into booster categories. Conversely, "every new meta entrant has at least 2 currencies in addition to boosters" enabling reward diversity.

### Reward System Analysis

**Consumables vs. Currencies:**

| Aspect | Consumables | Currencies |
|--------|------------|-----------|
| **Pros** | Direct gameplay impact | Subjective player choice |
| **Cons** | Limited scaling | Inflation risk |

The analysis suggests both rarity systems and multi-currency approaches could substantially improve player motivation as meta features accumulate.

## Meta Match 3: Barriers to Entry

### Competitive Landscape

**Early Adopter Dominance:**
- Playrix demonstrates aggressive marketing responses
- Referenced source notes: "Don't poke the Russian Bear"
- Proportional UA spend escalations create defensive barriers

**Vertical Integration Strategies:**
- AppLovin combines data analytics platforms with game studios (Belka, Firecraft)
- This merger creates "powerful strategy" linking industry data with internal development

**Consolidation Activity:**
- Zynga's acquisition of Peak Games (Toon Blast, Toy Blast) for $1.8 billion signals resource concentration
- Frank Gibeau emphasized data science scaling benefits

### Market Positioning

Despite apparent barriers, opportunities emerge through audience diversification rather than direct genre competition.

## Genre Blending: Property Brothers Home Design

Storm 8's Property Brothers represents innovative category expansion targeting mature audiences beyond traditional Match 3 demographics.

### IP Strategy and Market Fit

The television show Property Brothers targets:
- Women: 47% audience share
- Millennials (ages 26-40): significant percentage
- Baby boomers (ages 45+): strong demographic

This audience composition aligns directly with "Thinker" player archetypes in meta Match 3 games, creating natural overlap.

### Core Gameplay Loop

Property Brothers establishes narrative progression through client-based decoration challenges. Key differentiator: "Property Brother offer way more choice in decoration meta and refreshed UX as players are not linearly restricted to just upgrading one home."

Rather than single property progression, players engage with diverse homes (living rooms, bedrooms, nurseries) representing varied aspirational scenarios.

### Educational Narrative Integration

Narration combines fictional storytelling with interior design facts, creating educational value. This "mashing of interior design facts with narrative fiction naturally appeal to players who love interior design."

### Collapse Mechanics Adoption

The game employs collapse rather than swipe mechanics—a strategic shift evidenced by comparing Storm 8's earlier swipe-based decoration game against the collapse-based Property Brothers. Chart data demonstrates the "COLLAPSE avatar is finding way more success than its identical SWIPE cousin."

#### Addressing Board Stalemate Issues

Collapse mechanics introduce "Board Stalemate" design problems where impossible board states prevent completion. Property Brothers mitigates this through:

1. Larger board dimensions
2. Frequent "super gems" (bombs, dynamite, barrels) enabling strategic combinations
3. Delayed hard levels (60+ threshold) allowing skill development

Comparable titles employ similar solutions: Lily's Garden uses expanded boards; Toon Blast delays difficulty curves beyond 100 levels.

### Multi-Currency Event Design

The game implements clever time-limited mechanics:

- **Regular coins** limited to interior decoration
- **Time-limited tokens** exclusive to specific events
- **Dual-value structure** enabling progress across both gameplay modes simultaneously

This architecture "naturally incentivises players towards playing events" since event participation doesn't diminish regular progression.

## 2020 Genre Trends and Conclusions

1. **Classic Match 3 barriers increase** as King pivots Candy Crush toward meta-driven evolution

2. **Meta Match 3 barriers remain elevated** through early adopter aggression, vertical integration, and consolidation activity

3. **Gateway audiences from adjacent casual genres** (fashion, cooking, narrative fiction, farming) represent breakthrough opportunity

4. **Currency system modernization** beyond single-economy models enables reward scaling alongside feature expansion

5. **Core thesis:** "Experimentation, finding new audiences and genre blending is going to be the key to evolving the genre & reaping rich rewards, despite high barriers to entry."

---

## 2020 Prediction Validation Update (December 1, 2020)

Project Makeover launched November 2020, combining fashion, makeup, dress-up, and interior decoration elements. The title achieved #12 top-grossing status within launch month, validating the article's prediction regarding "audiences from other casual genre" success and genre-blending viability.
